### Problem

A blogging product should feel responsive and scalable without depending on long-lived servers or expensive hosting overhead.

### Approach

I architected the app around an edge-first backend and stateless request handling:

- deployed the backend on Cloudflare Workers to eliminate traditional server management
- designed stateless REST APIs with JWT validation at the edge using Hono.js
- integrated Prisma with PostgreSQL under serverless constraints and handled connection limitations cleanly
- paired the backend with a React, Recoil, and Vite frontend for a responsive publishing workflow

### Outcome

The platform proved that an edge-native architecture could lower cost, reduce latency, and still support a practical full-stack developer experience.
